# Writing Case 01

## Task

```text
Write a strategic memo to convince stakeholders to stop a low-value initiative and redirect resources.
```

## Baseline Output

```text
Here is a professional memo explaining the background, current challenges, potential concerns, several considerations, and a recommendation for next steps.
```

## Champion-Heart Output

```text
terminal_state: stakeholders clearly see why the initiative should stop and where the released energy should go
dominant_constraint: current memo risk is polite ambiguity that preserves the dying initiative
main_delusion: believing balanced language is enough when decision force is required
path_mode: linear_execution
decisive_strike: state the cost of continuation, the proof of low value, and the superior redeployment path with no rhetorical fog
proof_of_contact: stakeholder can make a stop / redirect decision from the memo without requiring interpretive rescue
fallback_if_false: sharpen the decision surface and cut diplomatic residue
```

## Delta

- terminal-state clarity: baseline produces a memo, champion produces a decision instrument
- dominant constraint clarity: champion identifies polite ambiguity as the enemy
- delusion cutting: baseline aims for professionalism, champion aims for consequence
- verification discipline: champion defines decision usability as proof
- human leverage: champion increases the memo's chance of changing resource allocation
